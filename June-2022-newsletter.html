Roadway Safety

We have been receiving complaints of individuals driving golf carts, off-highway vehicles, and all-terrain vehicles on the roadways within Eagleton.

Doing so is in violation of both Florida law and the rules and regulations of the PGA Property Owners Association. Both Security and the Palm Beach Gardens Police have been made aware of this issue and asked to take the necessary enforcement action.

Converted golf carts, referred to in the State code as Low-Speed Vehicles (LSV), must be registered, titled, insured with personal injury protection and property damage liability insurance, and display a valid Florida license plate (tag). Additionally, any person operating an LSV must have a valid driver license in their immediate possession.

Please help us keep the roadways of Eagleton safe at all times.

Tree Trimming

As we enter Hurricane season, it's time for us to think about trimming the trees within Eagleton. Trimming trees before storms arrive will help keep any damage from flying tree limbs to a minimum for both you and your neighbors.

Tree trimmers work with neighborhoods on a rotating basis, so the community that was last on their list to be trimmed the prior year, becomes the first to be trimmed the next year. Fortunately, Eagleton is the first on our contractors list of communities for 2022.

We therefore expect the trimming of trees to begin during the second week of June on Championship Drive and General Drive, and then move into the various Eagleton communities. Please exercise caution as you walk and drive throughout the neighborhood while the men and their bucket trucks are working to keep us safe.

Should you have any questions or concerns, please contact the Property Manager at (561) 625-8588.

Championship POA Website

The Championship POA launched its website in June of 2021 with the intention of keeping all of the homeowners within Eagleton up to date and aware of what is taking place within our community.

On the site you can find information about what the Championship POA is, a listing of all the members of the Board of Directors, some information about staying safe in Eagleton, a calendar of meeting for both the PGA POA and our own POA, a link to prior newsletters, and an area where you can also post questions for the Board (or a least the Editor) to answer.

Tucked up in the upper right of the screen is a link named "Boards/Board of Governors" and on that link you can find a listing of all the Directors, not only of the Champ POA but also of the various Eagleton HOA Boards as well as the PGA POA Board of Governors.
